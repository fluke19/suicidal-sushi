britbet

The previous company I worked for (i-neda) recommended me for a contract role that was 3 months but I was kept on until 5.

The client is an up and coming tote betting site which won the rights for the UK tote and needed a designer to work with i-neda to create a new betting platform from scratch.

I largely worked remotley with the product lead but I met up once a week with him and the shareholders to present my designs/ideas for the site.

I thought it was best to go for a clean, modern design to not clutter all the data. The interface especially had to be intuitive, fast and simple to use to allow punters to put their bets on in time.

It was tricky displaying that amount of data whilst not completley overloading the customer with information, I felt having solutions like table rows which could open and close was a elegent way of hiding less useful data whilst making it obvious where it is if needed.

The site will be live in some point in July (hopefully!) here. I built and designed the landing pages that are currently there.

Suaposta

EJF Search

I do freelancing in my spare time and was asked to create a new site for an executive search company. They simply wanted a website that illustrated what they are about and what they do.

At first the client explained to me they had issues with a different web designer, where they had handed them a template and asked for them to fill in the information. When the client said they didn't have all the information the web designer dropped them and they came to me to see if I could do anything about this.

I first looked at the content and there wasn't a lot of it, so I suggested we go for a single page website and I crafted the wordpress theme from scratch to suit the content and what they wanted to say.

The colours and logo were already provided but I liked the purple and felt like it suited the professional, sophisticated and premium feel they wanted, so I kept to a clean and modern style and chose fonts to match that.

The site is live here.

Dar Zemora

I was recommended to this client by the client at EJF Search.

The client has a highly rated boutique hotel in Morroco but their website was atleast 5 years old and they wanted a re-design. They were hoping to get a more modern looking website whilst giving off that premium feeling.

Like EJF Search they didn't have a great deal of content and told me they wanted a site that only had the essentials and allowed the user to quickly look through the information. I suggested to go for a single page website and have the "book now" link fixed in the header to allow the user to book at any point when browsing the website.

One of the most challenging parts of the project was having a gallery on a single page website, I couldn't find any website out there which dealt with that problem, but I feel breaking the different sections into single thumbnails and having the galleries in separate lightboxes was a pretty good solution that I am proud of.

The site is not live but I have it hosted here. I am currently waiting for more assets but it should be finished soon!
